RESEARCH PAPER // TIER 2

Towards a Pre-Linguistic Inference Engine

SUBJECT: A Cross-Disciplinary Synthesis

ABSTRACT

This paper proposes a theoretical framework for pre-linguistic inference—a mode of reasoning that operates beneath the symbolic layer of language. Drawing from cognitive science, neuroscience, and AI research, we explore how pattern recognition, spatial reasoning, and embodied cognition can inform the design of next-generation inference engines.

"Pre-linguistic inference represents the substrate of thought that exists before verbalization—the intuitive, geometric, and relational processing that underlies all higher-order reasoning."

CORE PRINCIPLES

  • // P1: Spatial-relational reasoning precedes symbolic representation
  • // P2: Embodied cognition informs abstract concept formation
  • // P3: Pattern recognition operates across sensory modalities
  • // P4: Inference emerges from structural harmonics, not semantic labels
